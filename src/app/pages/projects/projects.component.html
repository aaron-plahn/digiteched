Digital Morphological Dictionary Project

Tools: SQL, ffmpeg, Sox, Mozilla DeepSpeech, Amazon Transcribe, NodeJS, Python

Developed and implemented a rapid audio clip creation pipeline that leverages English speech-to-text, which we have used to record around 250,000 words and phrases to date.

Language Mobile Apps

I designed, developed, and published the following mobile apps. I also did the audio processing. I stitched the graphics together using CC0 and stock artwork to supplement my own photography.

Digital Verb Book

Tools: NativeScript, NodeJS, SQLite

This first version contains about 7,000 words and phrases organized into 'third person singular' paradigms. This was a minimal viable product, very dense in linguistic information. Our next generation morphological dictionary, currently in progress, app will feature roughly a quarter-million words and phrases organized into arbitrarily complex vocabulary lists.

Memory Match

Tools: NativeScript, Android, iOS

This app was an attempt to bring my long-time coworker Bella Alphonse's favorite classroom activity to the digital realm. It is a spin on the classic, with audio in Tŝilhqot’in. It is a favorite among kids.

Tŝilhqot’in Alphabet

Tools: NativeScript, NodeJS, Android, iOS

This app accomponies a beautiful classroom poster that doubles as the cover of Bella Alphonse’s practical introduction to the orthography. This project started with a request from teachers to modernize the classic, well loved alphabet poster.

Qungh ʔAnaghunt’in

Tools: GDevelop, Apache Cordova, HTML

I built and packaged this app over Christmas break a few years ago. A prototype in scope, it helped me to gather support for the project, leading to the apps above. In the future, we hope to expand this to a full feature language tutor. I designed many of the interactions to mimic natural langauge acquisition in Athabaskan.

Cosmological constraints on new scalar gravitational interactions

Tools: Fortran, Python, PCA, Fisher Forecasting, CAMB, Scientific Computing

I wrote custom patches to the numerical software Code for Anisotropies in the Microwave Background (CAMB) using Fortran.

The Physical Review D publication is available on Arxiv.org.

For the more visually inclined, checkout our graphs, featured in Physical Review D's 2016 Kaleidoscope.

Tools: SciLab, Maxima, Numerical Analysis

I held a TRU Undregraduate Research Experience Awards Program (UREAP) scholarship for my numerical work with Dr. Richard Taylor (TRU Mathematics and Statistics) on the PCR3BP of Celestial Mechanics.
